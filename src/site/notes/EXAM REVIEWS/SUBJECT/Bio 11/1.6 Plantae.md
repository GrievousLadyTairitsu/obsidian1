#  Plants
#### Plant Detail
- Primarily multicellular
- Most plants are autotrophic
- Always protected by a cellulose cell wall which is a biological polymer
	- Filamentous
#### Plant Classification
- Increases in complexity with presence of new types of tissues and organ systems
- **Seedless non-vascular plants 
	- Small plants with a large surface area 
	- Reproduce through spores and need a lot of moisture (more aqueous environments)
- **Seedless vascular plants
	- Very tall
	- Can reproduce with spores through the alternation of generations
- **Seed-generating vascular plants
	- Seeds fertilize with pollen 
		- Pollen fertilization often occurs through other organisms
	- Fertilized seeds become fruits
		- Dispersed through feces or general movement to settle in the ground
- Vascular systems
	- A circulatory system of tubes to carry nutrition and resources
#### Plants vs Algae
[[EXAM REVIEWS/SUBJECT/Bio 11/1.0p Protista\|1.0p Protista]]

| Charateristics      | Algae                                                | Seeded Plants                                             |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Environment         | Aqueous                                              | Terrestial                                                |
| Sufficiency         | Self-sufficient in new cells                         | Produces a new organism dependent on parent embryo        |
| Circulation         | No vascular tissue to transport water and nutrition  | Contains vascular tissue to transport water and nutrition |
| Reproductive Organs | Does not contain reproductive organs                 | Seeds and flowers for reproduction                        |
| Sexuality           | Asexual reproduction (exception of some green algae) | Can always undergo sexual Reproduction                    |
