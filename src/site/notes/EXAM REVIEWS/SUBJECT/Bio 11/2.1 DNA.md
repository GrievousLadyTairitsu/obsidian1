 - Pentose Sugar
	 - Structural backbone for the DNA
- Phosphate groups
	-  Transfers ATP, provides structure and information
- Sugar-phosphate Backbone
	- Pentose Sugar and Phosphate groups come together to form a stable backbone
- Nitrogenous Bases
	- These are the variations of the nucleotide. They may differ. The different bases are:
	- Adenine (A)
	- Guanine (G)
	- Cytosine (C)
	- Thymine (T)
- Nitrogenous Base Pairs
	-  Nitrogenous Bases always pair with one other base
		- A-T (2 Hydrogen Bonds)
		- G-C (3 Hydrogen Bonds)
	- The order doesn't really matter as long as these are always bonded to each other
#### DNA Mitosis 
- The DNA always comes in pairs. When undergoing mitosis, two identical DNA molecules are formed, half of them the new one and half of them the original.

- DNA during interphase (OUTSIDE of Mitosis) are known as **chromatin** (de-condensed free-floating DNA), also known as a non-duplicated Chromosome
- In preparation for mitosis, chromatin condense into **duplicated chromosomes**
![Pasted image 20260304230719.png\|630](/img/user/7%20-%20Images/Pasted%20image%2020260304230719.png)
#### Key Terms:
- **Chromatid** - One half of a replicated chromosome
- **Sister Chromatids** - A pair of two identical Chromatids
- **Chromosome** - A Condensed form of chromatin (when preparation for mitosis), consists of sister chromatids. Essentially a coiled molecule of DNA.
- **Duplicated Chromosome** - A duplicated chromosome consisting of a pair of Sister Chromatids joined together at a centromere.
- **Centromere** - An attachment point for spindle microtubules. It also holds together Sister Chromatids in a Duplicated Chromosome.
#### Central Dogma
- DNA (**deoxyribonucleic acid**) is copied into RNA (**Ribonucleic Acid**). Which are single nucleotide chains. This is done so that the DNA itself is not used.
- The cell uses the RNA to make proteins
	- Proteins make roughly 16% of the body yet they are used for:
	- Enzymes
	- Structure
	- Signalling
	- Transportation of nutrients
	- Antibodies
